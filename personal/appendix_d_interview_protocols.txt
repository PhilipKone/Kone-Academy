Appendix D: Detailed Interview Protocols and Qualitative Instruments

D.1 Teacher Interview Protocol

D.1.1 Professional Development Experience Protocol

Opening Questions:
1. "Can you tell me about your previous experience with computing or technology before participating in the Kone Academy professional development?"
2. "What were your expectations when you first learned about this program?"
3. "How would you describe your comfort level with technology before this training?"

Professional Development Experience Questions:
4. "Walk me through your experience during the Kone Academy professional development sessions. What stood out to you?"
5. "Which aspects of the training were most valuable for your teaching practice?"
6. "What specific skills or knowledge did you gain during the PD?"
7. "How did the hands-on experience with the platform influence your confidence?"
8. "What challenges did you face during the professional development process?"
9. "How well did the PD prepare you for actual classroom implementation?"
10. "What would have made the professional development more effective for you?"

Implementation Preparation Questions:
11. "How did you prepare your classroom for implementing Kone Academy?"
12. "What concerns did you have about starting the implementation?"
13. "What resources or support did you feel you needed before beginning?"
14. "How did you plan to integrate the platform with your existing curriculum?"

Pedagogical Approach Questions:
15. "How has your teaching approach changed since implementing Kone Academy?"
16. "What teaching strategies have you found most effective with this platform?"
17. "How do you manage students with different skill levels in your classroom?"
18. "How do you balance technology use with traditional teaching methods?"
19. "How do you assess student learning when using the platform?"

Classroom Management Questions:
20. "How do you manage device access and sharing in your classroom?"
21. "What strategies do you use to keep students engaged during platform activities?"
22. "How do you handle technical problems during class time?"
23. "How do you maintain classroom discipline while students use devices?"

Student Interaction Questions:
24. "How have your students responded to using Kone Academy?"
25. "What changes have you observed in student engagement or motivation?"
26. "How do students interact with each other during platform activities?"
27. "Have you noticed differences in how boys and girls use the platform?"
28. "How do you support students who struggle with the technology?"

Curriculum Integration Questions:
29. "How do you connect Kone Academy activities to other subjects you teach?"
30. "What Ghanaian cultural elements have you incorporated into your teaching?"
31. "How do you ensure the platform activities are relevant to your students' lives?"
32. "How does this platform support Ghana's educational standards?"

Challenges and Solutions Questions:
33. "What has been your biggest challenge in implementing Kone Academy?"
34. "How have you overcome these challenges?"
35. "What unexpected issues have arisen during implementation?"
36. "How do you adapt when technical problems occur?"
37. "What strategies have you developed for working with limited resources?"

Support and Resources Questions:
38. "What ongoing support do you need for successful implementation?"
39. "How helpful has the technical support been during implementation?"
40. "What additional resources would help you teach computing more effectively?"
41. "How do you collaborate with other teachers using this platform?"
42. "What role does school administration play in supporting your implementation?"

Impact and Outcomes Questions:
43. "What changes have you observed in your students' learning since using Kone Academy?"
44. "How has this implementation affected your professional satisfaction?"
45. "What evidence do you see that students are developing computational thinking skills?"
46. "How has this experience influenced your view of technology in education?"

Sustainability and Future Questions:
47. "Do you plan to continue using Kone Academy after the research ends?"
48. "What would enable you to sustain this implementation long-term?"
49. "What advice would you give to teachers just starting with this platform?"
50. "How would you like to see this program evolve in the future?"

Closing Questions:
51. "Is there anything else about your experience with Kone Academy that you think is important for us to know?"
52. "What final thoughts do you have about computing education in Ghana?"

D.1.2 Interview Administration Guidelines

Pre-Interview Preparation:
- Schedule interviews at teacher's convenience (45-60 minutes)
- Ensure private, quiet location for interview
- Test recording equipment before interview
- Review teacher's background and implementation context
- Prepare interview guide with prompts and follow-up questions

Interview Process:
- Begin with informed consent procedure
- Explain interview purpose and confidentiality
- Use semi-structured approach allowing flexibility
- Record audio with permission
- Take brief notes for key themes and follow-up
- Maintain neutral, non-judgmental stance
- Allow teacher to guide conversation to important topics

Post-Interview Procedures:
- Thank teacher for participation
- Explain next steps in research process
- Transcribe recording within 48 hours
- Review transcript for accuracy
- Begin initial coding and theme identification
- Store data securely following ethical guidelines

D.2 Student Focus Group Protocol

D.2.1 Focus Group Structure and Guidelines

Group Composition:
- 6-8 students per focus group
- Balanced gender representation when possible
- Mixed ability levels within each group
- Same grade level or age range
- Familiar with each other but comfortable sharing

Facilitation Approach:
- Create relaxed, informal atmosphere
- Use age-appropriate language
- Encourage all students to participate
- Manage dominant personalities
- Use visual aids and examples when helpful
- Maintain focus on discussion topics

Session Structure:
- Welcome and introduction (5 minutes)
- Warm-up activity (5 minutes)
- Main discussion topics (30-40 minutes)
- Creative activity or demonstration (10 minutes)
- Closing and thank you (5 minutes)

D.2.2 Focus Group Protocol Questions

Opening and Warm-up:
1. "Let's go around and share your name and one thing you enjoy doing at school."
2. "What does a typical school day look like for you?"
3. "Have you used computers or technology in school before? What was that like?"

Platform Experience Questions:
4. "Can you tell me about using Kone Academy in your class? What is it like?"
5. "What activities do you remember most from using the platform?"
6. "What was your favorite thing about Kone Academy? Why?"
7. "What was challenging or difficult about using the platform?"
8. "How does using Kone Academy compare to other ways you learn in school?"

Learning and Understanding Questions:
9. "What new things have you learned while using Kone Academy?"
10. "Do you feel like you understand computing better now? How?"
11. "Can you give an example of something you learned that surprised you?"
12. "How do you feel when you figure out a difficult problem on the platform?"

Engagement and Motivation:
13. "What makes you want to keep using Kone Academy?"
14. "How do you feel when it's time to use the platform in class?"
15. "Do you ever use the platform or think about it outside of class?"
16. "What would make the platform more fun or interesting?"

Cultural Relevance Questions:
17. "Do the examples and activities on the platform relate to your life in Ghana?"
18. "What Ghanaian things have you seen on the platform that you liked?"
19. "Are there things about your community or culture you'd like to see on the platform?"
20. "Do you feel proud when using the platform? Why or why not?"

Collaboration and Social Learning:
21. "Do you work with other students when using Kone Academy? How?"
22. "How do you help each other learn?"
23. "Have you taught someone else something you learned on the platform?"
24. "What do you learn from your classmates during these activities?"

Gender and Inclusion:
25. "Do you notice differences between how boys and girls use the platform?"
26. "Do you think everyone in your class gets to participate equally?"
27. "Are there students who find it easier or harder to use the platform?"

Future Aspirations:
28. "Has using Kone Academy made you more interested in computers?"
29. "Would you like to learn more about programming or technology?"
30. "Can you imagine using computers in your future job?"
31. "What technology skills would you like to learn next?"

Creative Activity:
32. "If you could add any activity to Kone Academy, what would it be?"
33. "Draw or describe your ideal computer learning activity."
34. "What problem in your community could you solve using computers?"

Closing Questions:
35. "What advice would you give to other students just starting to use Kone Academy?"
36. "Is there anything else you want to tell us about your experience with the platform?"
37. "What should we know to make the platform better for students like you?"

D.2.3 Focus Group Facilitation Guidelines

Creating Safe Environment:
- Establish ground rules for respectful discussion
- Ensure all students feel comfortable sharing
- Use icebreakers to build group comfort
- Address any power dynamics within group
- Maintain positive, encouraging atmosphere

Managing Discussion:
- Use open-ended questions to encourage detailed responses
- Follow up on interesting comments with probing questions
- Balance participation across all group members
- Redirect conversation when it strays off topic
- Use visual aids or examples to clarify concepts

Handling Challenges:
- Manage shy students by providing multiple participation opportunities
- Guide dominant students to allow others to speak
- Address any conflicts or disagreements constructively
- Adapt questions based on student responses and engagement
- Maintain flexibility while covering key topics

D.3 Classroom Observation Protocol

D.3.1 Observation Framework and Structure

Observation Dimensions:
- Teacher behaviors and instructional strategies
- Student engagement and participation patterns
- Technology use and technical interactions
- Classroom management and organization
- Learning interactions and collaboration

Data Collection Methods:
- Structured observation checklist (quantitative)
- Narrative field notes (qualitative)
- Time sampling for engagement patterns
- Video recording (with permission)
- Student work samples and artifacts

Observation Schedule:
- Pre-implementation baseline observation
- Weekly implementation observations (30-45 minutes)
- Special focus observations (technical challenges, group work)
- Post-implementation comparison observation

D.3.2 Detailed Observation Checklist

Teacher Instructional Behaviors:
- Clear explanation of learning objectives
- Appropriate pacing for student understanding
- Effective questioning techniques
- Individual student support and differentiation
- Technology integration with pedagogy
- Classroom management during technology use
- Assessment and feedback provision

Student Engagement Indicators:
- On-task behavior frequency (recorded every 5 minutes)
- Voluntary participation and contribution
- Help-seeking behaviors and persistence
- Peer interaction and collaboration
- Creative problem-solving attempts
- Questioning and curiosity demonstration
- Emotional engagement (enthusiasm, interest)

Technology Use Patterns:
- Device access and rotation effectiveness
- Technical problem resolution approaches
- Software feature utilization
- Student technical proficiency development
- Offline vs. online functionality use
- Navigation and interface interaction

Learning Interactions:
- Teacher-student interaction quality
- Student-student collaboration patterns
- Peer tutoring and knowledge sharing
- Gender interaction dynamics
- Cross-cultural interaction observations
- Problem-solving discussion quality

Classroom Environment:
- Physical arrangement for technology use
- Noise level and focus maintenance
- Resource availability and accessibility
- Safety and equipment care
- Cultural relevance of materials and examples

D.3.3 Observation Protocol Implementation

Pre-Observation Preparation:
- Review lesson plan and objectives
- Prepare observation tools and checklists
- Coordinate with teacher about observation purpose
- Test recording equipment if used
- Arrive early to observe setup and preparation

During Observation:
- Maintain unobtrusive presence
- Record systematic observations using checklist
- Take detailed narrative notes on significant events
- Note time stamps for important occurrences
- Document unexpected events or challenges
- Record student quotes and interactions verbatim when possible

Post-Observation Procedures:
- Review and complete observation notes
- Conduct brief informal debrief with teacher
- Identify themes and patterns for analysis
- Store observation data securely
- Schedule follow-up observations if needed

D.5 Data Analysis and Coding Framework

D.5.1 Qualitative Data Analysis Approach

Coding Strategy:
- Use both deductive and inductive coding approaches
- Develop initial codebook based on research questions
- Allow emergent codes to arise from data
- Use multiple coders for reliability
- Maintain audit trail of coding decisions

Analysis Software:
- NVivo or similar qualitative analysis software
- Systematic organization of data sources
- Cross-case analysis capabilities
- Visualization tools for pattern identification

Reliability and Validity:
- Inter-coder reliability checks (κ > .80)
- Member checking with participants
- Triangulation across data sources
- Peer debriefing with research team
- Reflexivity journal maintained by researchers

D.5.2 Thematic Codebook Structure

Implementation Themes:
- Teacher preparation and confidence
- Student engagement and motivation
- Technical challenges and solutions
- Pedagogical adaptation strategies
- Classroom management approaches
- Support system effectiveness

Cultural Context Themes:
- Cultural relevance and adaptation
- Local language integration
- Community connection and relevance
- Ghanaian educational context
- Infrastructure constraints and adaptations

Learning and Development Themes:
- Computational thinking skill development
- Problem-solving approaches
- Collaboration and peer learning
- Creativity and innovation
- Confidence and self-efficacy development

Equity and Access Themes:
- Gender participation patterns
- Socioeconomic considerations
- Geographic and rural-urban differences
- Disability and inclusion considerations
- Resource distribution and access

Sustainability Themes:
- Teacher continuation intentions
- Institutional support and commitment
- Resource requirements for scaling
- Policy alignment and support
- Community acceptance and support
