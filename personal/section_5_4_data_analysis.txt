5.4 Data Analysis

Data analysis integrates quantitative and qualitative approaches to generate comprehensive understanding of Kone Academy implementation, examining both learning outcomes and implementation processes. This section details the analytical procedures applied to quantitative outcome data, qualitative experiential data, and the mixed-methods integration that synthesizes across data types.

**Quantitative Analysis**

Quantitative data analysis examines learning outcomes, engagement patterns, and attitudinal changes through statistical procedures appropriate for the multi-level data structure and research questions.

Pre/post assessment analysis employs repeated-measures analysis of variance (ANOVA) to examine changes in computational thinking scores from baseline to post-implementation, with school and classroom as grouping variables. Effect sizes (Cohen's d) quantify the magnitude of learning gains, providing standardized measures of outcome magnitude comparable across studies. Subgroup analyses examine whether learning gains differ by gender, grade level, prior technology exposure, or geographic context, identifying potential equity concerns or differential effectiveness.

Hierarchical linear modeling (HLM) addresses the nested data structure where learners are nested within classrooms nested within schools. HLM estimates outcome variance attributable to different levels (learner characteristics, classroom implementation, school context) and models cross-level effects such as how school-level infrastructure conditions moderate individual learning gains. This multi-level approach appropriately handles data dependencies that violate standard regression assumptions while enabling examination of contextual influences on outcomes.

Item response theory (IRT) analysis examines how individual assessment items function across the ability spectrum, identifying items that may function differently for learners with different background characteristics (differential item functioning). IRT provides item-level diagnostics that inform assessment refinement and ensure that outcome measurement is valid across the diverse learner population.

Learning analytics analysis employs descriptive and inferential statistics to characterize engagement patterns, examine relationships between engagement and outcomes, and identify predictors of persistence or dropout. Time-series analysis examines engagement trajectories over the implementation period, identifying patterns of sustained engagement, early dropout, or late disengagement that inform understanding of implementation sustainability.

Correlation and regression analyses examine relationships between implementation variables (fidelity, intensity, duration) and learning outcomes, identifying critical implementation components for effectiveness. Mediation analysis examines whether attitudinal changes (interest, self-efficacy) mediate relationships between platform exposure and learning outcomes, testing theoretical models of how Kone Academy produces educational effects.

**Qualitative Analysis**

Qualitative data analysis employs systematic coding procedures to identify patterns in implementation experiences, stakeholder perspectives, and contextual influences, generating theoretical understanding grounded in empirical data.

Thematic analysis organizes qualitative data (observation field notes, interview transcripts, focus group recordings) through iterative coding procedures. Open coding identifies discrete concepts and phenomena in raw data, with codes emerging from data content rather than predetermined categories. Axial coding relates codes to one another, identifying relationships, hierarchies, and dimensions of variation. Selective coding integrates codes into overarching themes that capture core patterns in the data. This iterative process moves between data immersion, coding, and theme development until theoretical saturation is achieved.

Coding procedures employ both manual analysis and computer-assisted qualitative data analysis software (CAQDAS) to manage the substantial qualitative dataset. Two analysts independently code subsamples of data, with inter-coder reliability checks ensuring coding consistency. Disagreements are resolved through discussion and codebook refinement, establishing shared interpretive standards before full dataset analysis.

Constant comparison techniques examine similarities and differences across cases (schools, teachers, learners), identifying patterns that hold across contexts versus phenomena specific to particular conditions. Cross-case analysis reveals common implementation challenges and effective practices while identifying contextual factors that shape implementation processes.

Analytical memo-writing documents interpretive thinking throughout analysis, recording emerging insights, alternative interpretations, and questions requiring further data examination. These memos become part of the audit trail documenting analytical decisions and supporting claims about findings.

Member checking involves returning interpreted findings to participants for validation, ensuring that analytical representations authentically reflect participant experiences and perspectives. Feedback from member checking informs analysis refinement and strengthens claims about finding credibility.

**Mixed Methods Integration**

Integration of quantitative and qualitative findings generates comprehensive understanding that neither approach alone could achieve. The convergent parallel design employs simultaneous quantitative and qualitative data collection, with integration occurring during interpretation where findings from each strand are compared and contrasted.

Joint display tables organize quantitative results and qualitative themes side-by-side, enabling direct comparison and identification of convergent, complementary, or discrepant findings. Convergent findings—where quantitative outcomes and qualitative experiences align—strengthen confidence in conclusions. Complementary findings—where qualitative data elaborate quantitative patterns—provide depth and explanatory mechanism. Discrepant findings—where quantitative and qualitative results diverge—signal complexity requiring nuanced interpretation.

Explanatory sequential analysis uses qualitative findings to explain quantitative results, particularly unexpected or ambiguous patterns. When quantitative analysis reveals outcome variation that statistical models cannot explain, qualitative data illuminate contextual factors or implementation processes accounting for variation. Similarly, when quantitative results show effectiveness, qualitative data reveal how that effectiveness is achieved in practice.

Expansion occurs when qualitative findings identify phenomena not captured by quantitative instruments, generating new understanding that extends beyond initial research questions. These expansions may inform subsequent research or platform refinement, demonstrating how mixed methods generate comprehensive knowledge.

**Validity and Rigor**

Analytical rigor is ensured through multiple strategies appropriate to each data type and the mixed-methods integration.

Quantitative rigor employs established statistical assumptions checking, model diagnostics, and sensitivity analyses. Assumption violations are addressed through data transformation or alternative analytical approaches. Multiple imputation addresses missing data under missing-at-random assumptions, with sensitivity analyses examining whether conclusions hold under different missing data assumptions.

Qualitative rigor follows established criteria for trustworthiness: credibility (confidence in findings' truth), transferability (applicability to other contexts), dependability (consistency over time), and confirmability (neutrality with respect to findings). Strategies ensuring these criteria include prolonged engagement with data, persistent observation, triangulation across data sources and methods, peer debriefing, thick description enabling transferability judgments, and audit trails documenting analytical decisions.

Mixed methods rigor requires integration quality—whether quantitative and qualitative components are effectively combined to generate comprehensive understanding. Integration strategies are explicitly documented, with attention to whether mixing occurs at design, methods, interpretation, or reporting stages. The meta-inference quality—conclusions drawn from integrated findings—is evaluated for coherence and defensibility.

**Conclusion**

The data analysis strategy employs quantitative statistical procedures, qualitative thematic analysis, and mixed-methods integration to generate comprehensive understanding of Kone Academy implementation. This analytical approach enables rigorous examination of learning outcomes, rich description of implementation processes, and integrated insight into how the platform functions across diverse Ghanaian educational contexts. The commitment to analytical rigor ensures that research findings provide valid foundations for both practical platform refinement and theoretical contribution to educational technology knowledge.
